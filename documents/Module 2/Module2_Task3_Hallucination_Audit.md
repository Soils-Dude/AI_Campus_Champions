## Reflection: which type was hardest, and why

The hardest hallucinations to catch were the **extrinsic, contextually-plausible** ones: a fabricated but authoritative-sounding citation ("a 2019 WWF report") and quantitative figures wrapped in accurate context ("over 400 billion trees," "30% deforested"). They are dangerous precisely because they are fluent and domain-adjacent — they look right to a non-specialist and survive a normal read. The Marie Curie category-swap (Physics ↔ Chemistry) was a close second: each fact is individually true, so only the *pairing* is wrong, which a reader checking facts one at a time can miss. The defense is mechanical claim extraction before verification, and treating every named source and specific number as guilty until independently confirmed.
